Bio

I started out in the newbie zone in Santa Barbara, California. I had min-maxed my stats to be very intelligent, both mentally and emotionally, but balanced out with asthma, which was extremely physically limiting, and depression, which was emotionally and mentally limiting. Fortunately, my randomly generated party was made up of two amazing parents and four incredible siblings.

Before I had finished the newbie zone, my parents relocated the party up to the Oregon coast to a small town called Tillamook. I finished up the newbie zone right around the age of 18, and decided level in college next. College didn't go so well. The problem was that I was more focused on leveling my emotional skills in relationsps than I was in improving my mental skills in classes. That the college leveling zone was so focused on mental skills made this nearly impossible. I went back to the newbie zone to figure something else out. I repeated this mistake several times.

It ended up taking me several years of on-and-off courses to gradute college, but I finally did it. I got a B.S. in philosophy, which had thoroughly taught me that reason and argument are equally as effective and valid as physically forcing someone to do something; in general, both are simply matters of convincing the other person that the argument or fight isn't worth the trouble. This doesn't determine who is right; only who is more skilled at argument/fighting.

I continued leveling my emotional skills as much as possible. There were many adventures and failures, some worthy of becoming stories, some less so. It wasn't until I started to notice that no one else was leveling alongside me that I realized that I had very nearly reached the maximum level my current environment had to offer. It was also around that time that I discovered that emotional skills, no matter how high level, do not make a lot of money. At least, not without the appropriate corrosponding mental skills.

That brings us to now. Now, I'm grinding my mental skills in web engineering in order to reach a place of financial stability, at which point I can more easily access higher level content.
